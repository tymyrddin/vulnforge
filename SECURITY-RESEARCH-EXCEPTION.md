# Security research exception

Notwithstanding the [Polyform Noncommercial Licence](LICENCE), the author explicitly permits the use of this software 
for legitimate security research purposes. Given that the entire point of this tool is to find vulnerabilities in code, 
this exception covers a lot of ground.

## Permitted activities

The following activities are explicitly allowed without a commercial licence, provided they are conducted in good faith:

- Vulnerability research and analysis
- Development and testing of proof-of-concept exploits
- Defensive research and mitigation testing
- Red team and blue team exercises where no fee is charged
- Academic research and publication
- Incident response preparation and tabletop exercises
- Demonstrations intended to improve security awareness

This includes running the tool against codebases you own or have permission to audit, integrating it into research 
pipelines, and adapting the prompts or pipeline stages for your own research context.

## Responsible use

The exception applies to good-faith research. That means operating on codebases you own or have permission to test, 
following responsible disclosure practices for any findings, and not using the tool to assist attacks against systems 
you are not authorised to touch.

## What this exception does not allow

- Paid security services
- Commercial penetration testing or compliance auditing
- Consultancy engagements
- Training delivered for a fee

If research transitions into a paid engagement, a commercial licence is needed.

## Intent

The intent of this exception is to raise the level of security, not to restrict it. If you believe your use case 
aligns with this intent but falls into a grey area, [reach out](https://tymyrddin.dev/contact/).
